Walmart Supplier Center Content Guidelines — Electronics

PRODUCT TITLES
Walmart product titles may be up to 200 characters. Unlike Amazon, Walmart's search algorithm places significant weight on the first 75 characters of the title — ensure the most important keywords and the brand name appear early.

Required elements: brand name, product name, key feature or model identifier. Optional but recommended: size, color, count, or variant information.

Walmart prohibits: "sale," "clearance," "free," pricing information, delivery promises, or retailer-specific terms. Superlatives ("best," "greatest") are discouraged but not automatically suppressed — however, unsubstantiated claims can result in listing removal.

PRODUCT BULLETS / KEY FEATURES
Walmart allows up to 5 feature bullets. Unlike Amazon's 255-character limit, Walmart's bullet character limit is 500 characters per bullet. This allows sellers to include more specification detail per bullet point.

Walmart recommends a "feature + benefit + specification" structure: state the feature, explain why it matters to the customer, and include the supporting specification. Example: "Active Noise Cancellation — block out distractions in open offices or noisy commutes with 3 adjustable ANC levels that reduce ambient noise by up to 35dB."

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION
Walmart supports product descriptions up to 4000 characters — significantly more than Amazon. Use this space to create a comprehensive product story. Include a brief intro paragraph, a spec summary, compatibility information, and what's included in the box.

Walmart's algorithm rewards richer, longer descriptions that fully describe the product. Thin descriptions (under 500 characters) may result in reduced search visibility.

SEARCH KEYWORDS
Walmart's keyword field supports up to 1000 characters. Include all relevant synonyms, alternate product names, use-case terms, and compatible device categories. Walmart's search algorithm is more forgiving about keyword repetition than Amazon's, but focus on relevance over volume.

ELECTRONICS SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
For consumer electronics on Walmart:
- Always include connectivity specifications (Bluetooth version, range)
- Always include battery specifications (capacity in mAh or runtime in hours)
- Include dimensions and weight for portable/wearable devices
- Include compatibility information prominently
- Include all accessories included in the box
- IP or water resistance rating should appear in bullets if applicable

CONTENT QUALITY AND COMPLETENESS SCORE
Walmart measures listing quality through the Content Score — a 0-100 metric visible in the Supplier Center. Items below 60 receive reduced search placement. Items above 80 are eligible for premium placement in search results and category pages.

Key factors in Walmart Content Score: image count and quality, title completeness, bullet count and length, description length and completeness, category attribute completeness.

For electronics, completing all category-specific attributes (connectivity type, battery life, dimensions, compatibility, color) can add 15-20 points to the Content Score above what title and bullets alone provide.
