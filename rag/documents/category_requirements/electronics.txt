Electronics Category Content Requirements — Cross-Retailer Guide

HEADPHONES AND AUDIO EQUIPMENT

Title Best Practices:
The most effective headphone titles on Amazon follow this structure:
[Brand] [Model Name/Number] [Product Type] [Key Feature 1], [Key Feature 2], [Color/Variant]

Examples of high-performing title structures:
- "Sony WH-1000XM5 Wireless Industry Leading Noise Canceling Headphones with Alexa Voice Control, Black"
- "Bose QuietComfort 45 Bluetooth Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones — Over Ear Headphones with Microphone, Up to 24 Hours Battery"

Key terms that drive search volume in the headphones category:
- "wireless headphones" (380,000+ monthly searches)
- "bluetooth headphones" (450,000+ monthly searches)
- "noise cancelling headphones" (290,000+ monthly searches)
- "over ear headphones" (210,000+ monthly searches)
- "headphones with microphone" (180,000+ monthly searches)
- "active noise cancellation headphones" (95,000+ monthly searches)

Required specifications for headphones listings:
1. Bluetooth version (e.g., Bluetooth 5.0, 5.2)
2. Battery life in hours (playback, with ANC on/off if different)
3. Charging time to full
4. Driver size in mm (for over-ear and on-ear)
5. Connectivity options (USB-C, 3.5mm, wireless only)
6. Microphone type and count (built-in, beamforming, etc.)
7. Weight in grams
8. Foldable: yes/no
9. Multipoint pairing: yes/no and number of devices
10. Water/sweat resistance: IP rating if applicable
11. Compatibility: iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, gaming consoles

Bullet Point Strategy for Headphones:
High-performing headphone listings consistently use these 5 bullet structure:
1. AUDIO QUALITY — driver specs, frequency response, tuning philosophy
2. NOISE CANCELLATION — ANC type, effectiveness, adjustable levels
3. BATTERY AND CHARGING — total hours, fast charge capability, standby
4. CALL QUALITY — microphone count, type, call clarity features
5. COMFORT AND DESIGN — weight, ear cushion material, foldability, accessories

Headphone Content Mistakes to Avoid:
- Claiming "industry leading" without substantiation — major brands (Sony, Bose, Apple) own this space
- Vague battery claims ("long battery life") without specifying hours
- Missing Bluetooth version — critical purchase signal for buyers
- No IP rating mention when the product has one — buyers specifically filter for this
- Describing fit as "one size fits all" — technically inaccurate and weakens trust
- Using "crystal clear" or "crisp audio" without supporting specifications

SMART SPEAKERS AND HOME AUDIO

Title Requirements: Include voice assistant compatibility (Alexa, Google Assistant, Siri) if applicable. Include key room coverage spec if marketed for home use.

WIRELESS EARBUDS

Additional Required Specs: ear tip sizes included, active vs passive noise isolation, IP rating for sweat resistance (critical for fitness marketing), stem vs compact form factor, charging case battery capacity.

GENERAL ELECTRONICS QUALITY STANDARDS

The minimum content threshold for competitive placement in Electronics:
- Title: 80+ characters, contains brand + product type + 1-2 key features
- Bullets: exactly 5, each 150+ characters, each covering a distinct product dimension
- Description: 800+ characters, covers use cases and key specs not in bullets
- Backend keywords: 200+ characters, no title repetition

Listings meeting all four criteria score in the top 30% of their category on average quality metrics.
