Amazon Product Listing Style Guide — Electronics Category

TITLE REQUIREMENTS
Titles must be between 20 and 200 characters. Include the brand name, product type, and key differentiating features. Use title case — capitalize the first letter of each major word. Do not use ALL CAPS. Include the model number when it helps customers identify the correct product.

Prohibited in titles: "best," "#1," "guaranteed," "free shipping," "sale," "deal," "limited time," "today only," "act now." Do not include promotional language of any kind.

Required in Electronics titles: brand name, product type (e.g., "Wireless Headphones," "Smart Speaker," "Bluetooth Earbuds"). Model number strongly recommended. Key specs that differentiate the product (e.g., "Noise Cancelling," "30-Hour Battery," "Active Noise Cancellation") may be included if they fit within the character limit.

BULLET POINTS
Electronics listings require exactly 5 bullet points. Each bullet point must be 255 characters or fewer. Begin each bullet with a capital letter. Do not begin bullets with symbols, special characters, or promotional language.

Structure bullet points around customer benefits, not just features. Lead with the benefit, then support it with the feature or specification. Example: "Stay connected all day — 30-hour battery life means fewer interruptions on long commutes or work-from-home sessions" is stronger than "30-hour battery life."

Do not include pricing, shipping information, or promotional offers in bullet points. Do not include HTML tags in bullet points.

PRODUCT DESCRIPTIONS
Product descriptions may be up to 2000 characters. Limited HTML is permitted: <b> for bold, <br> for line breaks, <li> and <ul> for lists. Use HTML sparingly — not every sentence needs formatting.

Focus descriptions on use cases, key benefits, and what makes the product the right choice for the customer's situation. Avoid repeating what is already in the title and bullets verbatim.

Prohibited in descriptions: "best seller" (unless substantiated with a citation), "top rated" (without proof), "award winning" (without specifying the award), vague lifetime warranty claims without specific terms.

BACKEND KEYWORDS
The search terms field allows up to 250 characters. Do not repeat keywords already in the title. Do not include competitor brand names. Do not use commas — separate keywords with spaces. Do not include prohibited words or offensive terms.

Use backend keywords to capture alternate phrasings, synonyms, and use-case terms that could not fit in the title or bullets. Example: if your title says "Wireless Headphones," use backend keywords for "cordless headphones," "over ear headphones," "on ear headphones," "headset."

CONTENT QUALITY STANDARDS
Amazon evaluates listing quality across multiple dimensions: keyword relevance, content completeness, and customer experience. Listings with incomplete content are less likely to appear in search results and are more likely to be suppressed.

Common reasons for suppression: missing required fields, title exceeding character limit, insufficient bullet points, prohibited content.

Electronics Category Specific Requirements:
- Include connectivity specifications (Bluetooth version, Wi-Fi standard, etc.)
- Include battery life in hours or mAh for battery-powered devices
- Include compatibility information (iOS, Android, Windows, Mac)
- Include dimensions and weight for wearable or portable devices
- Include water resistance rating (IP rating) if applicable
- Include warranty information in the description (not in title or bullets)

KEYWORD STRATEGY
High-performing Electronics listings on Amazon target 8-12 relevant keywords across title, bullets, and description combined. The most valuable keywords appear in the title. Secondary keywords can appear naturally in bullets and description.

For headphones and audio equipment, top search terms include: bluetooth headphones, wireless headphones, noise cancelling headphones, over ear headphones, headphones with microphone, active noise cancellation headphones, foldable headphones.

Keyword stuffing — repeating the same keyword multiple times unnaturally — can suppress listings and should be avoided.
